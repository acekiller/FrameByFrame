Commands and Shortcuts

This page summarizes the commands and features of FrameByFrame and their corresponding keyboard shortcuts.

FeatureButtonDescription
Taking a picture or press ⌘-.
You can set a custom shortcut in the Preferences; the default is ␣ (space bar) on the keyboard, play button on the remote.		Creates a picture from the current video input and inserts it before the picture selected in the picture strip.
Deleting picturesDeletes the picture(s) selected in the picture strip.
Navigating picturesDefaults are ←, →, ↑ and ↓ for previous, next, last and first picture. You can customize these in the Preferences.
Use +, -, ↞, ↠ on the remote.		Using these you can move the selection through the picture strip.
Setting the frames per secondUse slider.You can choose a frame rate of the final movie ranging from 1 to 30 frames per second.
Setting onion skinning opacity and for 0% or 100% opacity; use slider for values in between.Determines onion skinning opacity in the preview view (current camera image will overshadow selected picture).
Preview moviePlays the movie starting with the selected picture.
Saving the movieChoose "File > Save", or press ⌘-S.Saves the movies in FrameByFrame's native format.
Exporting the movie in QuickTime formatChoose "File > Export...", or press ⌘-ESaves the movie as a QuickTime movie which can be viewed by many applications.
Quick exportChoose "File > Quick Export...", or press ⌘-⇧-ESaves the movie in QuickTime format using a default codec (H.264 or MPEG-4).
Adding pictures from outside FrameByFrameChoose "Edit > Import...", or press ⌘-IAdds pictures from locations outside FrameByFrame at the end of the movie.
